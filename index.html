A little about me...

I am a Lactation Consultant (IBCLC), Certified Lactation Educator, and hold a Bachelor of Arts Degree in Spanish from the University of Minnesota, Morris. I received a Lactation Consultant degree from the University of California, San Diego, and completed a clinical internship at Hennepin County Medical Center in Minneapolis, Minnesota. I am also CPR/AED/BLS/First aid certified. I visit mothers and fathers in the Minneapolis/Saint Paul and surrounding areas to address breastfeeding concerns.

When I'm not helping mothers breastfeed, I can be found on my bike, on my sailboat, in the garden, in the kitchen baking, or traveling. I've been interested in sustainability for decades and find breastfeeding to be an element in creating a sustainable life. I am the mother to a very clever and sweet 4 year old daughter who is the light of my life. I have a blog too.

I offer three types of consultations: In addition to home visits, I am at Willow Midwives one day per week and Hennepin County Medical Center's Breastfeeding Clinic three days per week. The latter options offer insurance coverage with most insurance plans. You may call Willow/HCMC directly or contact me to schedule an appointment.

Breastfeeding can awaken both positive and challenging emotions. My intention is to help mothers realize their breastfeeding goals in a gentle and supportive manner, while practicing evidence-based techniques. Breastmilk is the optimal nutrition source for your baby, and it is an important component of their development. 

Lactation Consultation

If you're experiencing: soreness, latch issues, weight gain concerns, suspected thrush/plugged ducts/mastitis, tongue/lip tie, oral restriction/anatomy issues, milk supply concerns, prematurity, multiples, traumatic birth, returning to work, exclusive pumping, or have any other concerns that are affecting your breastfeeding relationship, please consider a consultation.

A lactation consultation can help parents make informed and conscientious decisions on what is best their family regarding infant nutrition and breastfeeding. 

Elements of a Consultation

Our initial consultation will allow me to gather information about your breastfeeding situation. It is best scheduled during a time that your baby will want to breastfeed, so I can fully observe a feeding and address any issues and concerns you may have. Initial consultations range from 1-2 hours and include follow up phone and email support. Follow up visits are available as well.

A consultation usually starts by discussing the health and birth history. From there we'll address any concerns, observe a feeding, check oral anatomy if necessary, and create a care and feeding plan together. 

Partners/family/support people are welcomed and encouraged to join. Please have any feeding accessories (nipple shields, pillows, breast pump, feeding log, cup/bottle, supplementation, etc) available during my visit.
