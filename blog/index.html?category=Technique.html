The One Thing Every Breastfeeding Mother Should Learn

November 23, 2014

A common question I receive is, "What's the one thing you would tell any breastfeeding mother?" and while there are a lot of things I'd like to tell breastfeeding mothers, there is one thing that I'd like them to learn: Hand Expression. 

Maybe it's my general affinity for a simple, old-fashioned way of doing things, but hand expression is perhaps one of the most useful tools in maximizing milk production and can be used instead of an electric breast pump in an emergency. It can also be useful for expressing milk in the first few days when breasts can be too swollen, and colostrum can be quite thick. An electric pump is sometimes useless in these situations. There are many instances where hand expression works as well, if not much better than electric or manual/hand pumps in expressing milk. Hand expressing is also useful if your electric pump breaks, if you find yourself separated from your baby longer than you had expected (and didn't bring your pump), if you need to be discreet (or at least without the hum of an electric pump), or if you're simply tired of cleaning pump parts. It also stimulates your breast in a way that is more natural than a plastic pump flange and many women find it more productive than a breast pump.
